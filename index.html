  • My
    Story
    Begins

  • 1995-

    Greater Chicago Area

    Stephen, raised in the suburbs of Chicago, is a technologist. Problem solver at heart, he looked towards development as a career. Though he spent his early years gaining basic information technology experience, and rudimentary software development skills, he has since shifted his career towards Robotic Process Automation. Currently his latest ventures have been developing his skills as an RPA Developer, or Modeller.

  • June 2009-July 2019

    Civil Air Patrol

    One of the first ways Stephen gave back to his community was through volunteer work. He started volunteering at the Elgin chapter of the Civil Air Patrol as a flight member. At the Elgin Squadron, Stephen gained the foundations of his Search and Rescue training. Eventually the Elgin Squadron shut down due to lack of participation, and Stephen then transferred to a different Squadron. The last few years of his volunteer service was carried out at the Fox Valley Composite Squadron as one of the Deputy Commander of Cadets. He has since moved on from this particular organization due to a shift of priorities in his life.

  • 2010 - 2014

    Travels

    Part of being in the Civil Air Patrol is attending encampments and training camps as a cadet. During that time as a cadet Stephen visited multiple camps. His favorite was Cadet Officer School. He learned what it meant to be a leader at Maxwell Air Force Base Gunter Annex.

  • Fall '11 - Fall '13

    ECC

    After completing his highschool curiculum Stephen moved from doing school at his house to commuting to a college. He admitted himself into the Elgin Community College to work on getting his Associates Degree. Making it a general degree so that he would have freedom moving forwards!

  • January 2014 - January 2020

    Army National Guard

    After graduating with an Associates in Science, Stephen was not quite sure what he wanted to do. So without wasting any time he visited the Army recruiters. After scoring a 90 on his ASVAB and doing his research he settled on the Illinois Army National Guard as an Information Technologies Specialist. He worked as support for tactical internet setups and equipment as part of the 406th Brigade Signal Company.

  • December 9, 2014

    Graduation Two

    Advanced Individualized Training, the second part of training that typically comes after boot camp, where an individual such as Stephen learns how to do his Military Occupational Specialty or job. Stephen obtained a Information Technology Specialist Deploma after attending about 20 weeks of studying.

  • Spring '15

    All or Nothing

    Knowing that the calculus classes at ECC differred from other colleges and Universities, Stephen opted to complete the series and transfer all 3 calculus classes instead of having to retake them at a different instituion. A choice that would pay off in the end.

  • Fall '15

    University

    Stephen studied for a Bachelor's degree in Computing Science. During this time period he had learned about multiple different languages, getting a rudimentary training in SQL query creation, along with the foundamentals of how to program in both Functional and Object Oriented languages, such as C and C++. You can learn more about this in the Educational Section. He Graduated in May of 2019

  • Spring '17

    CS251-TA

    During the Spring Semester of 2017 Stephen held a TA position for CS251 while working on his studies at UIC.

  • 2017 - 2018

    nBotix

    Spring Break of 2017 Stephen studied a software named Blueprism. This gave him the oppurtunity to get hired as a part time Junior Developer at nBotix. He quickly showed his worth and was promoted to Training Developer. His first major Business trip was to train Analysts with an nBotix partner.

  • Fall '17, Spring '18

    CS211-TA

    Aftering being a Teaching Assistant for 251, he decided that it would be beneficial to try and TA for a class that required students to work together on development. He did so by working as a TA for 211, allowing him to share some of his knowledge and experience to less experienced individuals.

  • June 1, 2018

    Blue Prism Certified Developer

    After working with nBotix for about a year, Stephen passed his first Blue Prism Certification Exam. He had already had experience with some projects with nBotix, and had done several proof of concepts using the Blue Prism Platform. This was essentially Stephen's way of providing proof that he knew a bit about the Blue Prism Software Platform.

  • June 7, 2018

    Blue Prism Certified Solution Designer

    After successfully passing his Blue Prism Certified Developer Exam, Stephen immediately scheduled another exam for the following week. Completing that exam with ease as well. After that point he took up another project for nBotix and became too busy to schedule another exam.

  • Spring '19

    CS362-TA

    After dealing with an nBotix project and schooling at the same time, Stephen opted out of attempting to do the same thing for his final semester at UIC. Instead he chose to TA for CS362-Computer Design. At some point throughout the semester, realizing he was the TA with the most experience of working with circuits and micro-computers, he stepped up and played a major role in the adminstration of the weekly labs.

  • May 12, 2019

    Graduation Three

    Stephen Graduated, opting not to walk or deal with the cerimonial process this time. The Bachelor's Degree took some time to arrive, and as such Stephen would not know for certain that it arrived until late June of 2019.

  • September 28, 2019

    Blue Prism Certified Professional Developer

    Although Stephen could have taken this exam 6 months after the Blue Prism Certified Developer exam, he had been busy with projects, and dealing with his final courses. He also put in some work attempting to help a failed RPA initiative before finally settling down and finding a Job. Stephen currently works as an RPA Developer or RPA Solution Designer in the Chicago Greater Area.
